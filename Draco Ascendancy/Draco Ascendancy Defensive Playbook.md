## PLAYBOOK 1: **Loss-Minimization Defense**

*(Core Draco defense — use this often)*

**Trigger Conditions**

* Neighboring faction applies pressure
* You cannot decisively win the next fight
* Your economy cannot support a prolonged war

**Threat Model**

* Attritional pressure meant to exhaust you
* Opponent wants you bleeding without payoff

**Primary Objectives**

* Survive engagements cheaply
* Preserve fleet cores
* Avoid decisive losses

**Immediate Actions (Next 1–2 Rounds)**

* Pull fleets back to defensible positions
* Consolidate ships into fewer engagements
* Avoid multi-front exposure

**Tech Priorities**

* Hull upgrades
* Computers (accuracy > damage)
* Energy efficiency

**Fleet Posture**

* Smaller, tougher fleets
* Designs that survive one bad roll
* Retreat-capable configurations

**Diplomatic Actions**

* Signal willingness to disengage
* Encourage conflict elsewhere
* Avoid escalation language

**What NOT to Do**

* Counter-attack emotionally
* Chase damaged fleets
* Fight without upside

**Exit Condition**

* Opponent shifts targets
* Table instability increases
* New combat opportunities emerge elsewhere

## PLAYBOOK 2: **Conflict Redirection Defense**

*(Draco’s strongest non-combat defense)*

**Trigger Conditions**

* Table aggression is focused on you
* Another faction is equally or more threatening
* You are being labeled “the problem”

**Threat Model**

* Coalition pressure
* Preemptive containment

**Primary Objectives**

* Stop being the focal point
* Turn attention outward
* Preserve midgame combat relevance

**Immediate Actions**

* Avoid initiating fights
* Publicly frame other threats
* Move fleets defensively, not aggressively

**Tech Priorities**

* Neutral survivability tech
* Avoid flashy or threatening upgrades

**Fleet Posture**

* Defensive stacking
* Fleets visible but inactive
* Border deterrence only

**Diplomatic Actions**

* Offer support against third parties
* Encourage “necessary” wars elsewhere
* Accept temporary non-aggression

**What NOT to Do**

* Prove your strength
* Finish tech tracks publicly
* Launch punitive strikes

**Exit Condition**

* Another faction becomes primary threat
* Table focus shifts
* New profitable fights appear

## PLAYBOOK 3: **Border Elasticity Defense**

*(Defending without holding everything)*

**Trigger Conditions**

* Strong neighbor pushing borders
* You cannot afford to defend all sectors
* Territory loss is imminent

**Threat Model**

* Slow territorial erosion
* Pressure meant to corner you

**Primary Objectives**

* Preserve fleets and economy
* Trade space for time
* Set up counter-extraction later

**Immediate Actions**

* Abandon low-value sectors
* Fortify key positions only
* Reposition fleets inward

**Tech Priorities**

* Mobility (drive)
* Hull > shields
* Moderate weapons

**Fleet Posture**

* Mobile reaction forces
* Avoid static defenses
* Strike only when trades are favorable

**Diplomatic Actions**

* Frame retreats as strategic
* Encourage overextension
* Keep channels open

**What NOT to Do**

* Die defending worthless space
* Split fleets thin
* Attempt full border holds

**Exit Condition**

* Opponent overextends
* Their economy strains
* You can extract value via counter-fights

## PLAYBOOK 4: **Late-Game Point Preservation**

*(When Draco is already ahead or competitive)*

**Trigger Conditions**

* Final 1–2 rounds
* You have meaningful point lead
* Combat opportunities are drying up

**Threat Model**

* Desperate denial attacks
* Endgame kingmaking pressure

**Primary Objectives**

* Protect existing points
* Avoid catastrophic losses
* Deny cheap scoring to others

**Immediate Actions**

* Reduce combat exposure
* Consolidate fleets defensively
* Pass earlier than usual

**Tech Priorities**

* Defensive tech only if immediate
* No speculative upgrades

**Fleet Posture**

* Defensive stacks
* Border denial
* No deep strikes

**Diplomatic Actions**

* Signal exhaustion
* Offer stability
* Avoid provoking endgame fights

**What NOT to Do**

* Hunt one more fight
* All-in attacks
* Overbuild fleets

**Exit Condition**

* Game ends
* Final scoring locked

---

## Meta-Rule for Draco Defense (Memorize This)

> **If a fight does not generate points, position, or future fights — it is a bad defensive action for Draco.**

Draco defense is **selective disengagement**, not resistance.
